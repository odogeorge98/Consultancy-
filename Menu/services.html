Our Services

The most popular export support service we provide is how to export goods from the UK to the EU including Ireland.

Since Brexit, exporting goods to Europe from the UK has become more complicated as we are no longer part of the single customs union and so export and import customs declarations are required. To complete these correctly you need to use the correct commodity codes, understand Incoterms and make sure all the relevant information is included on your commercial invoice. This is where we can help. If you’ve never exported before we will ensure all your information is correct, find the best logistics partner for you and write a simple process map for you to follow. We are always on hand to answer any questions that arise during the process of exporting your goods. Alternatively, if you’re an experienced exporter to the EU who is suddenly experiencing a new problem, we will find a solution. So whatever your level of expertise in exporting goods from the UK to Europe and Ireland or Northern Ireland, contact us today for a free consultation and let us help you grow your business.

Inward / Outward Processing

If your supply chain involves importing and exporting you may be paying duty unnecessarily. Raw materials, parts, components etc. that are imported to be manufactured into a final product for export may be exempt from import duty and VAT. Likewise, if you export a partially manufactured item to be processed and then bring it back to the UK to be finished you’ll pay duty at re-import on charges made for repair or replacement, plus any inward shipping and insurance costs you pay for the return of the goods but not on the value of the goods themselves.

Importing Goods

Importing any product into the UK can be complicated and there are a lot of things you need to get right before you start. 2024 is going to see a lot of changes relating to how goods are brought into the UK so it’s important to stay up to date to ensure that you comply with the latest legislation.
